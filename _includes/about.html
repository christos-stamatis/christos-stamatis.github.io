About Me


I am a chemical engineer trying to understand how our atmosphere works and how we affect it. My work focuses on the development of algorithms for pattern recognition analysis of wildland fire smoke and faster processing of two dimensional gas chromatography with time-of-flight mass spectrometry (GCxGC-TOFMS) data using Python.

Currently I am pursuing my Ph.D (5th year) in Chemical and Environmental Engineering from the University of California, Riverside (UCR) under the supervision of Dr. Kelley Barsanti.

I recieved my diploma in Chemical Engineering (emphasis in Environmental Engineering) at the University of Patras in Greece in 2016. My undergraduate thesis under the supervision of Dr. Spyros Pandis focused on regional air quality modelling with PMCAMx.
